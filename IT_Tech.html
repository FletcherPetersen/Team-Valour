Elisha Rackley - Cyrpto Currencies and Blockchain.

What is Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies

Blockchain is a digital database, also known as a Distributed Ledge Technology (DLT), which uses three main concepts: blocks, miners and nodes (BuiltIn, 2013). The blocks contain data and are digitally chained together, hence the name ‘blockchain’. Every time a new block is generated, a nonce (a 32-bit whole number) is generated with it. If it is the first block in a chain, the nonce generates a 256-bit number called a cryptographic hash which stays with the chain permanently. Miners then add to the initial block by trying to find an accepted hash using intense software to attempt to find one in the four billion different nonce-hash combinations. Should someone want to go back and ‘edit’ a block, they have to ‘re-mine’ that block and every single block after it. Not only that, but because blockchain is not just on one computer or one server but rather on nodes-which are any electronic device that has a copy of the blockchain, the blockchain is decentralised which means the blockchain is not stored in just one place (BuiltIn, 2013).

Crypto currency is a form of digital currency that uses this blockchain technology because it is decentralised and thus, more secure and trustworthy as no entity can manipulate it (Frankenfield 2020). However, blockchain is not limited to cryptocurrencies and can be used more broadly as an electronic ledger or record keeping system that cannot be manipulated or destroyed. These ledgers would be secure, reliable and safe from security breaches or hacks (BuiltIn, 2013).

The most popular cryptocurrencies used on exchange markets are Bitcoin(BTC), Ethereum(ETH) and Ripple (XRP). Bitcoin is considered to be the ‘original’ and most well know crypto currency which was launched in 2009. What makes cryptocurrency so incredible is that, as it uses blockchain technology, it is next to impossible to be manipulated by entities, such as governments, in the way fiat currency can. Cryptocurrencies, like Bitcoin and Ethereum, are decentralised currencies as many crypto currencies are, but there are still cryptocurrencies, like Ripple, which are centralised systems (Maloney, 2017).

In order to comprehend what makes cryptocurrency so revolutionary, one must understand currency why our currency should be a decentralised system as opposed to the current centralised systems that is just creating global debt from printing more currency than we can ever pay back. Back before paper currency was standard, gold and silver were used as currency as they were a finite resource that was given value. Eventually, the gold standard was introduced. Instead of paying for items or services with physical gold, you could store the gold with a bank and get a receipt for that gold. If someone was to then take the receipt to the bank, they could collect the physical gold. This paper receipt became our money but then the terrible decision was made to no longer have gold back these paper receipts so there is no value to the money other than what we give it. As everyone knows, the more there is of something there is, the less value it has; so when more money gets printed, the less value the money that is currently circulating has. This is also known as inflation and the reason for global debt, because the money that is printed, is meant to be paid back so that our paper currency does not just keep losing value like it currently is (Maloney, 2013).

Cryptocurrencies that are decentralised, like Bitcoin, are like gold as they have a finite value and cannot lose value by a government deciding to just create more bitcoin which is why cryptocurrencies are so radical and revolutionary (Maloney, 2017).

Cryptocurrencies, like Bitcoin, are slowly being used at more and more retailers. For example, cheapair.com is a travel agency that allows you to purchase everything for your holiday, including flights and accommodation, with Bitcoin and has been accepting Bitcoin as a form of payment since 2013 (Medal, 2017).

Cryptocurrencies have the capacity to replace banks as transactions will not need banks to oversee every transaction thus making your funds more secure and allow for faster transactions (Council, 2018). Another aspect of replacing banks would mean that governments would be unable to freeze or even access your crypto currency wallet as they would freeze your bank account. Replacing banks would also eliminate the problem of banks creating inflation. As there are two major ways inflation and debt is caused and created, the first being when more money is printed and put into circulation, the second is when that money reaches a bank. If you were to go deposit one hundred dollars at the bank so your bank account had one hundred dollars in it, but the bank only has to keep 10% of that on hand so they can go and give out that ninety remaining dollars if they so please, but your bank account still says one hundred dollars (Maloney, 2013).

Blockchain also has the capacity to improve Cybersecurity for companies and government by preventing unauthorized access and changes as all the data in a blockchain is verified and encrypted using cryptography. This would also mean that Government corruption and fraud would be drastically reduced if blockchain was utilised as there would be greater transparency when it comes to where Government funding goes to (Council, 2018).

Fletcher Petersen - Autonomous Vehicles.

What Are Autonomous Vehicles and What Do They Do?

An autonomous vehicle, or self-driving car, is a vehicle that uses a variety of sensors to move through the environment safely with almost no human control. There are no full-autonomous vehicles on the market at the present time but there is a wide range of vehicles that can use autonomous features to make driving safer and easier on the user. Full-autonomous driving would involve a vehicle that can operate on its own in all types of weather and driving conditions without any human input or intervention. This would be considered level 5 autonomy. The levels are determined the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE) International.

Level 2 would be considered state of the art as it is where you will find today’s autonomous vehicles, such as vehicles using Tesla’s AutoPilot system and Cadillac Super Cruise. This level of autonomy allows for a vehicle to use an array of cameras and sensors to keep the car at a certain speed and remain in the correct lane while remaining at a safe distance from other vehicles. Elon Musk, CEO of Tesla, stated while accepting the Axel Springer Award "I am extremely confident of achieving full autonomy and releasing it to the Tesla customer base next year" (Axel Springer SE, 2020). There is a ‘Full Self-Driving Beta system’ available to some Tesla owners, however this is not level 5 autonomy with user input still required. Musk also said he believes “10 years from now almost all cars will have full-autonomy.”

The technology involved in autonomous vehicles includes cameras, radar, lidar, ultrasonic sensors, GNSS, and more (Dawkins, T, 2021). Cameras can be used to send what they see to an AI-based program to determine what objects are around. This is great for daytime driving but will become less useful when visibility is low. Radar is used in most vehicles now to determine distance between the vehicle and approaching objects. This is necessary for autonomous vehicles to be able to stop when approaching an object or vehicle. Radar will usually use 2 frequencies, either 77GHz or 24GHz. 77Ghz is better for long-range sensing while 24Ghz is best for close range sensing. However, radar is not optimal for object detection. Lidar is a great sensor for autonomous vehicles as it emits a laser that reflects off the objects it hits and is received by a photodetector. This allows for a three-dimensional image of the environment to be processed, which is essential for the vehicle to determine the objects in its environment with the use of an AI algorithm. Ultrasonic sensors are what is currently used for parking sensors. This is useful for autonomous vehicles so they may park themselves, which is a feature used today by some higher-end vehicles.

What Is the Likely Impact of Autonomous Vehicles?

Impacts of autonomous vehicles may include changes to the economy, various industries, insurance, safety, and commuting (Ohio University, 2020). Autonomous vehicles remove the need for a person behind the wheel of the vehicle. This means there is a lot of potential job loss. In America, one in eight jobs have driving as an essential task for workers, there are more than 3 million professional truck drivers, and more than 10 million Americans hold Commercial Driver’s Licenses (CDL) of some kind (Zurschmeide, 2016). This could mean millions of people to lose their jobs, or have reduced responsibility, and therefore have considerable pay cuts. With the loss of jobs and pay there is potential for a recession, affecting the driver’s economy.

Another potential impact of autonomous vehicles in the insurance industry. Insurance company may have to ‘shift the core of their business model, focusing mainly on insuring car manufacturers from liabilities from technical failure of their AVs, as opposed to protecting private customers from risks associated with human error in accidents’ (Bertoncello and Wee, 2015, para. 8).

Autonomous vehicles will also change the way we think about commuting. The average daily commute time in 2017 was 66 minutes, increased from 49 minutes in 2002 (Ma, 2019). Autonomous vehicles will allow for commuters to spend that time relaxing or doing something else rather than focusing their attention on driving. Autonomous vehicles may also reduce the average commute time. If a fleet of autonomous vehicles communicates and works together it can improve traffic flow by at least 35% (ScienceDaily, 2019). Autonomous vehicles communicating with each other also improve safety when a human driver who was driving aggressively was added. The cars were able to give way to avoid the driver. This not only increases the commuting experience but also safety when the road is filled with autonomous vehicles, and human drivers.

What are small computing devices and what can they do?

  

Small computing devices like raspberry pi & arduino, which can be generalised as single-board computing, are small devices that are used to process data in a cost-effective and space saving manor. Single-board processing devices have aided the influx of smart-home devices, drones & data collection.

Small computing devices can collect & process data from several other hardware units. For example a weather station that may get several different data points such as temperature, rainfall windspeed and humidity. All the data can be collected via various sensors, all connected to the small computing device.

Applications have been proposed to the possibility of low-cost hydrologic data monitoring in remote & data scarce regions through a single-board computer system. Arduino is mentioned as a potential low cost alternative through the use of open-source software to extend existing networks. (Silja V. Hund, 2016)

Single board computing devices are also being used as an alternative to traditional computer clusters. Raspberry pi units are relatively inexpensive making it easy to expand clusters.

What is likely to be able to do be done soon (say in the next 3 years)?

In 1980, Bernard M. Oliver wrote of the significant technological advances made in the reduction of size & cost of computers. He mentions in 1968 the cost of a single 64kb machine would cost $50,000 without any input or output devices. 12 years later, around the time the GUI reached the market, the same amount of money would buy you a machine with 256kb memory & 50mb storage. (Oliver, 1980)

Fast forward 40 years to 2020 where a Samsung Galaxy S20 has 31,250 times more memory than a computer of the time.

Given the above, it would be uneducated to assume that single-board computing devices will not decrease in size and increase in power in the near future, providing even more practical applications for future raspberry pi & Arduino models. This will contribute to a reduction in cost, making existing devices like robot vacuum cleaners, google home & Amazon Alexa more compact, efficient and affordable.

What technological or other developments make this possible? 

The invention of the microprocessor like the ARM Cortex found in the google home has made efficient processing of data capable in a very small format with minimal cooling required.

NAND flash memory is a type of non-volatile, rewritable memory that is directly attached to the board. The invention of rewritable flash memory allowed for programs to be stored directly on the chip and also advancements in solar panel efficiency
